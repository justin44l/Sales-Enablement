Your AI tool guide

Three tools, one sales cycle. Claude, Gong, and Salesforce each do different things — and because Claude and Gong can't currently be linked, knowing when to use which saves time and avoids duplication.

G
Gong
Passive call intelligence — works automatically from your recorded calls
AI call summaries with next steps — no manual effort
MEDDPICC tracking updated from call recordings — even without rep input
Auto-populates Salesforce event records from calls
Account strategy & handover documents generated from all call history
Historic call review for account context before meetings
⚠ Gong only works from what's been recorded. It can't prep you before a call, write proposals, or roleplay objections.
C
Claude
Active thinking partner — you bring the context, it sharpens your approach
Pre-call research, hypothesis building, and call briefs
Value-led demo scripts built from your discovery notes
Objection roleplay using the LAR framework
Proposals, follow-up emails, champion enablement messages
Deal pressure-testing and MEDDPICC gap analysis
Post-call self-coaching and reflection
⚠ Claude has no access to your calls, Salesforce, or Gong data. You must paste context in manually. Never include customer names or contract values.
SF
Salesforce
Source of truth — fed by Gong automatically, maintained by you
Event records auto-populated from Gong-recorded calls
MEDDPICC fields updated from Gong call analysis
Pipeline stages, deal notes & manual updates — your responsibility
Account context to copy into Claude for prep and analysis
Tableau dashboards for account tiering & whitespace mapping
⚠ Gong feeds Salesforce automatically for recorded activities — but you still own pipeline accuracy, stage progression, and deal narrative.
Because Claude and Gong aren't linked — here's the bridge
You can't point Claude at a Gong recording. But you can move intelligence between them manually — and the two tools are most powerful when used in sequence.

1
Poor

Competent

Advanced

Expert
